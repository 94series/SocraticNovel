**Figure Description:**
- Two concentric circles representing the conducting shells.
- The inner shell is labeled with a charge of \(-2Q\).
- The outer shell is labeled with a charge of \(+2Q\).
- Points A and B are located between the two shells.

---

**Explanation:**
The electric field inside a conductor is zero, and the electric field outside a uniformly charged spherical shell is the same as if all the charge were concentrated at the center. Therefore, the electric field at point A (inside the inner shell) is zero, and the electric field at point B (between the shells) points radially outward due to the positive charge on the outer shell. 

Thus, the correct answer is:
- Electric Field Vector at Point A: 0 (no vector)
- Electric Field Vector at Point B: ↑

However, since none of the provided options match this exact scenario, the closest option that aligns with the expected behavior is:

**B.**
- Electric Field Vector at Point A: ↑
- Electric Field Vector at Point B: ←

This option suggests a non-zero field at both points, which is not entirely accurate but is the closest given the choices. The correct interpretation would be that the field at point A is zero, and the field at point B points radially outward. 

Therefore, the most appropriate choice based on the given options is:

**B.**

But for a more precise understanding, the correct answer should be:

- Electric Field Vector at Point A: 0
- Electric Field Vector at Point B: ↑

Given the constraints of the provided options, **B** is the closest match.
